## Reddit — r/teachingabroad
*(Conversational, no hype, upfront about what it is)*

**Title:** I built a tool to help teachers evaluate international postings before they sign — salary data, cultural analysis, school diagnostics

---

After watching a few too many colleagues sign contracts they regretted, I spent the last few months building something I wish had existed when I started in international education.

The core idea is the "three-legged stool": every posting has three legs — **School** (leadership, culture, mission), **Place** (city, safety, lifestyle), and **Package** (salary, housing, flights, tax). The research on expat adjustment is pretty clear: you need at least 2 of 3 to be solid. Most people who regret a move overweighted the package and underestimated the school.

What it includes:
- **592 real salary records** from international educators — filterable by country, curriculum, and role, with warnings built in for entries that look like currency errors
- **A cultural diagnostic** — 8 questions that help you tell the difference between cultural friction (which improves) and structural problems (which don't)
- **A "My Move" predictor** — if you're thinking of relocating, it forecasts your stool scores at the destination using the salary data and Hofstede cultural research
- **A Classroom Guide** — 8 common classroom behaviour patterns explained through cultural research, with strategies
- **A shareable profile** — you can share your current stool scores as a link, which other teachers can view (clearly labelled as personal assessment, not verified school data)

It's free, no login, no tracking. All data is client-side.

Would genuinely appreciate feedback from people who've actually lived this — especially on the cultural dimensions analysis and whether the diagnostic questions feel accurate.

## LinkedIn
*(Professional, insight-first, slightly longer)*

---

Most international teachers who regret a move made the same mistake: they over-weighted the package.

Salary, housing, flights — these are easy to quantify and recruiters lead with them. What's harder to evaluate before you sign: the school's actual culture, the leadership's honesty, the city's liveability for your specific situation, and how much cultural distance you're signing up for.

I've been developing a framework I call the **three-legged stool** — School, Place, and Package. Based on the research on expatriate adjustment, you need at least 2 of 3 legs to be genuinely strong for a posting to be sustainable. One weak leg is survivable. Two is a posting most people eventually leave.

I've turned this into a free tool called **stool** — an intelligence platform for international educators:

→ 592 real salary records from educators in 97 countries (community-submitted, anonymised, with outlier detection built in)
→ A cultural analysis tool using Hofstede's dimensions — shows your specific gaps between home and current country, and forecasts them at a destination
→ A diagnostic that distinguishes cultural friction from structural problems
→ A move predictor that forecasts your three scores at a prospective destination
→ A classroom guide explaining student behaviour patterns through cultural research

It's free, no login required, and all processing is client-side.

If you work in international education — or advise people who do — I'd genuinely value your feedback.
