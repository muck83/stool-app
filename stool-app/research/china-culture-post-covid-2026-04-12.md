## D1 — Learning Philosophy (Confucian ethics, effort, memorisation)

**1. Resurgence of Confucian education in contemporary China: Parental involvement, moral anxiety, and the pedagogy of memorisation**
- Year: 2022
- Publication: *Journal of Educational Administration and History* (Taylor & Francis)
- URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03057240.2022.2066639
- Relevance: Directly addresses Confucian learning ethics, memorisation as pedagogy, and parental moral anxiety about character development (zuoren/becoming human). Shows how parents dissatisfied with exam-oriented state schools seek Confucian-based moral cultivation. Strong fit for D1 bridge paragraphs.

**2. Is Confucianism compatible with autonomous learning? An investigation of cultural influences on self-regulated learning in China**
- Year: 2025
- Publication: *Frontiers in Education*
- URL: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/education/articles/10.3389/feduc.2025.1591474/full
- Relevance: Examines tension between Confucian tradition and modern autonomous learning goals. Addresses mind vs virtue orientation and constraints on critical thinking — directly maps to D1 existing framework.

**3. Text memorisation: An effective strategy to improve Chinese EFL learners' argumentative writing proficiency**
- Year: 2023
- Publication: *Frontiers in Psychology* (PMC)
- URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10112396/
- Relevance: Challenges Western stereotype of rote memorisation; shows memorisation-before-understanding can coexist with genuine comprehension. Useful for D1 "IB connection" card — nuances the narrative.

**Supplementary — 吃苦 (chiku) philosophy:**
- "In Praise of Hardship, or the Labour-Schooling Poetics of Chinese Youth" — *Made in China Journal*, 2025: https://madeinchinajournal.com/2025/08/26/in-praise-of-hardship-or-the-labour-schooling-poetics-of-chinese-youth/

## D2 — Parent Communication (WeChat, expectations, COVID impact)

**4. Implementation and Challenges of Online Education during the COVID-19 Outbreak: A National Survey of Children and Parents in China**
- Year: 2021–2022
- Publication: *PLOS ONE / PMC*
- URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9334978/
- Relevance: National survey documenting parent-teacher communication via WeChat groups during remote learning. Shows parents' supervisory role, co-education partnerships, and widespread pessimism about online teaching quality. Documents shift in parent expectations during COVID. Strong D2 update.

**5. Chinese parents' perception of emergency remote K-12 teaching-learning in China during the COVID-19 pandemic**
- Author: Zhang, T.H.
- Year: 2021
- Publication: *ERIC*
- URL: https://files.eric.ed.gov/fulltext/EJ1303710.pdf
- Relevance: Directly addresses Chinese parents' perceptions of emergency remote schooling. Documents parent anxiety, evolving expectations, and shifts in home-school partnerships. Good pair with source 4 for D2.

**6. Chinese children's perspectives of long-distance familyhood via WeChat**
- Year: 2024
- Publication: *Taylor & Francis*
- URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/17482798.2024.2308096
- Relevance: Examines WeChat's role in family and educational communication. Relevant to understanding the WeChat parent group dynamic described in D2.

## D3 — Institutional Context (Double Reduction, Gaokao, parent expectations)

**7. Examining China's 'Double Reduction' Policy: Promises and Challenges for Balanced and Quality Development in Compulsory Education**
- Authors: Licui Chen, Shuangmu Lin
- Year: 2024
- Publication: *SAGE Journals*
- URL: https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/20965311241265123
- Relevance: Peer-reviewed analysis of the 双减 (shuangj ian) policy's shift from exam-oriented to quality-oriented education post-2021. Shows intent to reduce memorisation focus, documents implementation challenges and parent responses (including emergence of underground tutoring). Best single academic source for D3.

**8. Double Reduction Policy — impact on international schools**
- Source: International Education News + policy analysis
- Year: 2021–2023
- URLs: https://internationalednews.com/2021/11/03/surprise-controversy-and-the-double-reduction-policy-in-china/
- Relevance: Analysis of differential impact on international schools vs. public schools and tutoring centres. International K-12 schools relatively less affected but saw opportunity in quality-education positioning. Documents parent expectation shifts post-policy. Use alongside source 7.

**9. The discursive (re)positioning of the foreign teacher: Understanding changing teacher roles in China's internationalised school sector**
- Year: 2024
- Publication: *ScienceDirect*
- URL: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0883035524001691
- Relevance: Addresses foreign teacher marginalisation despite symbolic importance; covers impact of 2021 educational reforms on international school staffing and the evolving role of foreign teachers. Directly relevant to how teachers experience D3 institutional pressures.

## D4 — Ethical Leadership (academic integrity, guanxi, 面子)

**10. Academic Integrity in China: Challenges for Policy, Practice, and Quality Assurance in Higher Education**
- Year: 2024
- Publication: *Springer Nature / SpringerLink*
- URL: https://link.springer.com/10.1007/978-3-031-54144-5_175
- Also: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/378355593
- Relevance: Directly addresses guanxi-based authorship practices, relationship-building norms vs. Western integrity frameworks, and tensions between hierarchy/authority and individual accountability. Documents the gap between government policies (since 2009) and entrenched cultural practices. Only D4-specific source found for this pass — worth supplementing with fieldwork data.

## D5 — Student Behaviour (classroom quietness, question-asking anxiety, perfectionism, COVID impact)

**11. Post-pandemic shifts in online learning: motivation and engagement of Chinese students**
- Year: 2025
- Publication: *Educational Studies* (Taylor & Francis)
- URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/13803611.2025.2482618
- Relevance: Longitudinal study showing post-pandemic changes in motivation, engagement, and behavioural patterns. Compares intrinsic/extrinsic motivation with behavioural, cognitive, and emotional engagement shifts. Strong D5 update for the post-COVID classroom dynamic.

**12. Analysis of classroom silence behaviours among Chinese and Korean undergraduates**
- Year: 2024
- Publication: *PMC*
- URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12713118/
- Relevance: Quantitative analysis of classroom silence and reluctance to ask questions in Chinese and Korean students. Identifies speaking anxiety and contextual rigidity as strongest predictors. Shows COVID post-lockdown impact on self-efficacy and classroom participation. Strong fit for D5 and for cross-referencing Korea D5.

**13. Influence of the first wave of COVID-19 on Chinese students' psychology and behaviour**
- Year: 2024
- Publication: *PMC*
- URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11217512/
- Relevance: Documents COVID's psychological and behavioural impact on Chinese students — covers perfectionism, depression, anxiety, and changes in classroom engagement patterns. Use to update D5 "what this may feel like in your classroom" sections.

**14. Psychological detachment in Chinese higher education: a multitheoretical model of academic stress, cultural pressure, and coping resources**
- Year: 2025
- Publication: *Frontiers in Psychology*
- URL: https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2025.1647184/full
- Relevance: Addresses perfectionist tendencies, rumination, fear of underperformance, and cumulative academic stressors specific to Chinese students. Good theoretical grounding for D5 dimension card.

**15. Post-pandemic shifts in online learning (supplementary)**
- Same as source 11 — note both intrinsic motivation decline and increases in performance anxiety post-COVID, relevant to D5 behavioural patterns in IB classrooms.
